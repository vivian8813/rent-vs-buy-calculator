Rent vs buy in the U.S.: what usually drives the result

In the United States, the rent vs buy decision is often dominated by mortgage interest rates, selling costs (frequently agent commissions), and local property taxes. Your expected time in the home is usually the single biggest factor‚Äîshort stays often make renting cheaper, while longer stays can favor buying if equity and appreciation outweigh ownership costs.

How to use this USA rent vs buy calculator

  1. Start with your expected stay. Try 3, 5, 7, and 10 years to see how break-even changes.
  2. Set a realistic mortgage rate. Even small changes can shift the outcome.
  3. Don‚Äôt ignore selling costs. High selling costs often delay break-even.
  4. Use a conservative investment return. This represents opportunity cost of cash.

Common U.S. assumptions to sanity-check

  • Property tax varies widely by state/county and can materially change costs.
  • Maintenance scales with home price‚Äîolder homes often cost more to maintain.
  • HOA fees can be substantial in condos and some planned communities.
  • Rent growth matters most if you expect to rent for many years.
